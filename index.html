Register Now for the July 11-15 Summer Kickoff!

Early Registration Discount Available until June 17

Bluesoxx hosts computer science programs for high school girls in Vancouver, BC.  This summer, we are taking on new members with any experience level (including none), and moving forward together until everyone is able to create their own personal project using the Python programming language.

Example projects include computer-generated art, traffic analysis using google maps, creating (and breaking) cypher-based codes, simulations of animal populations, simple games, and many other possibiities according to personal interest.

The course will run five days from July 11 to 15, in Downtown Vancouver, and enrollers can choose from either the morning or the afternoon sessions, with each running for four hours per day.  Through the rest of the summer, optional weekly office hours will be held to help individual members complete their projects if necessary.The specific downtown venue (to be central, near transit) and a fee for covering costs (no more than $250 for the entire program) will depend on enrollment levels.  Please sign up as soon as possible, to facilitate planning—and to receive a small discount when enrolling by June 17.

Girls who will be enrolling in a Vancouver-area secondary school next year, or who have recently graduated, are elgible to enroll; the only required equipment is a laptop.  (Please contact the organizers at info@bluesoxx.com in case of financial hardship.)

About Bluesoxx

The Current Cohort

For May and June, Bluesoxx has been working through introductory lessons in graphics, programming languages, signal processing, networks, and security.   Congratulations are due soon for Anastasia, Christel, Emily, Jessica, Julia, Lovely, Madeleine, Nicole, Rachel, Sara, Sydney, and Tina, as their Stanford CS101 certificates of completion are almost in hand!


First Class Ambitions

We will definitely make some fun and simple demo projects.   But overall, the class is about real fundamentals in formal computer science, and professional-level programming.   We will also work on leadership, presentation skills, and health and fitness: less crafting-for-kids and more being-a-boss.

Getting it Done Together

The Bluesoxx program combines online materials from university-level computer science courses with personalized instruction by a local expert.   Most importantly, though, students work together to build and share expertise as a team, and take the lead in making this happen: not quite like joining a sorority, but more than just taking a class.

Real Accomplishments

The ability to use software, like the ability to read 300 years ago, is transforming assumptions about what people can accomplish.   Early birds who progress from computer-literate to best-selling-authors of computation are not just guaranteeing the income or lifestyle they want--they have the opportunity to shape the world.
